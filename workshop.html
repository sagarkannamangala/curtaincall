Curtain Call's performance classes and workshops are focused in building not only performance skills, but confidence, social skills, presentation skills, creative instincts, teamwork and leadership qualities. We cater to all skill levels whether the raw-beginner or experienced professional and offer a low-pressure environment where students are able to explore and develop creative techniques applicable to any performance situation. Classes range from six to fifteen students and run once a week for eight-week sessions fall, winter and spring. Winter and summer vacation programs are also available.


Scholarships are available for families in need. To apply, simply submit a copy of your most recent tax return, a letter of recommendation from a teacher, instructor, mentor or coach who has worked with your child, and a letter from you and/or your child stating why our workshop program may be beneficial to him/her. These items can be submitted to our Education Director Brian J. Bianco by email at brian@curtaincallinc.com, or by fax at 203-322-3656. For more information, call 203-329-8207 or email brian@curtaincallinc.com.


Curtain Call follows the Stamford Public Schools calendar as to vacation, holiday and weather-related closings. For emergency or weather-related closing information, watch our website, News Channel 12 or tune in to 1400 WSTC.

Acting for TV and Film
(ages 12-15)

Instructor: Lisa Lelas
Tuesdays, 4:15pm to 5:30pm
1/20, 1/27, 2/3, 2/10, 2/24, 3/3, 3/10, 3/17

Working in front of a camera is closer to reality than stage acting and requires its own, unique set of skills. In this eight-week workshop, you’ll build the basic skills necessary for acting and auditioning for television, film and commercials using actual network and film scripts. Explore how body and facial gestures convey important information to a viewing audience and how the camera lens can become your best friend. You’ll also receive tips for finding agents and booking professional work. Lisa Lelas is a popular CT acting coach and former NY casting director. She’s helped train thousands of actors who’ve attained professional work in TV, film and Broadway. Lisa is a motivational speaker, a performance coach for the Sacred Heart University’s Women’s Skate Team, the film-acting instructor for the annual Moondance International Film Festivals and the founding owner/director of the state’s first TV-acting studio. She’s a well-known author, newspaper/magazine columnist and was the organizing segment host for ABC-TV Channel 8. Lisa was also the creator/host of “Simply Organized” on Cable TV, for which she won an award for best talk show in the northeast by the Alliance for Community Media Foundation. www.LisaLelas.com $250
